You need real, big carrots for this, not the little "baby" ones that come in a bag.

Get about 3 big carrots and have your grown-up cut about 2 inches from each top. Pull off the old leaves, and stick the tops with their cut sides down in a container with small rocks--to hold the carrots up--and water. The water should cover the carrots about half-way. Put the container in a place that's bright but not sunny all the time, and not too hot.

In about a week, you should see little green leaves growing from the carrot tops. You can put it in a sunnier spot now, and make sure it always has enough water.

The problem with growing carrot plants this way is that it's hard for them to develop new roots, so the plant may not live for more than a few weeks. If it does develop new roots, you can plant it on soil and enjoy it for a longer time.
